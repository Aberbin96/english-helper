# Learning Path: B1 to B2 in 6 Months

This study plan is designed for working professionals. It maximizes dead time during the day and concentrates effort into strategic blocks, targeting the ~180 hours of exposure needed to reach B2 without overwhelming your daily routine.

---

## 📈 6-Month Roadmap (3 Phases)

### Months 1–2: Adaptation & Structure
Close the B1 gaps and absorb B2 grammar foundations. Expand formal vocabulary and train your ear to native speed. Subtitles in series or videos must switch to English only.

### Months 3–4: Active Immersion & Consolidation
Gradually reduce translation tools. By now you should understand the general context of complex conversations and radio programs without effort. Writing and speaking should begin to include synonyms and advanced connectors.

### Months 5–6: Strategy, Agility & Exam Prep
If your goal is an official certificate (Cambridge FCE, TOEFL, IELTS), focus on exam technique: optimize time per section, understand scoring criteria, and maximize fluency. Intense timed mock exam practice.

---

## 📅 Weekly Schedule

| Time Block | Activities | Skill / Goal |
|---|---|---|
| **Mon–Fri · Commute / Breaks** (20–30 min/day) | Active listening: BBC 6 Minute English or TED Talks. Short articles from international press (BBC, The Guardian). Note 2–3 new words per day. | **Listening & Reading** — passive immersion and vocabulary acquisition in real contexts. |
| **Tue & Thu · Evening Focus** (45–60 min/day) | Structured study: B2 grammar (mixed conditionals, passive voice, phrasal verbs, advanced connectors). Practical exercises in textbooks or digital platforms. | **Use of English & Grammar** — building the technical foundation and linguistic precision needed. |
| **Mon–Fri · Before Bed** (10 min/day) | Write a short diary entry of 4–5 lines using structures or vocabulary learned during the week. | **Writing** — ingraining writing habits and structuring thought in English. |
| **Saturday or Sunday · Production Session** (1.5–2 hours) | First hour: official exam simulations (complex readings or formal essays). Second hour: oral practice with natives (iTalki, Cambly), language exchange, or personal voice recordings on debate topics. | **Speaking & Exam Skills** — oral fluency, confidence, and time management under exam conditions. |

---

## 💡 Psychological Sustainability Rule

Studying while working full-time is a long-distance race. If a day's work fatigue is extreme, don't force grammar. Replace it with leisure in English — an episode of a series, a YouTube video on your favorite hobby, or music while analyzing the lyrics. Maintaining daily contact with the language is more valuable than forced memorization under stress.
