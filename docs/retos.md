# Progress Checkpoints: Stage-by-Stage Roadmap

> "Don't think about the full 6 months. Focus only on checking off the challenges of your current stage."

---

## Stage 1: Months 1–2 — "The Foundation"

**Goal:** Establish B2 grammar foundations and eliminate B1-level habits.

### Weekly Focus Routine

**Tue & Thu (45–60 min):** Master the key structures for this stage:
- **Narrative Tenses:** Fluid combination of Past Simple, Continuous, and Perfect.
- **Passive Voice:** Use in formal contexts and written texts.
- **Relative Clauses:** Advanced relative connectors to unify ideas.

**Weekends (Production):** Speaking practice centered on yourself — talk about your professional background, goals, and interests to loosen up.

### Mini Checkpoints

- [ ] **Challenge 1:** Switch your phone, computer, and social media language entirely to English.
- [ ] **Challenge 2:** Watch a full episode of a familiar series with English-only subtitles and follow the plot.
- [ ] **Challenge 3:** Write a formal email (workplace simulation) of 120 words using connectors like *However*, *Furthermore*, or *Therefore*.

---

## Stage 2: Months 3–4 — "The Immersion"

**Goal:** Move beyond everyday topics and step into abstract debates and advanced vocabulary.

### Weekly Focus Routine

**Tue & Thu (45–60 min):** Introduce complex grammar and agility:
- **Conditionals:** Deep study of the third conditional and mixed conditionals.
- **Modal Verbs of Deduction:** Natural use of structures like *must have been*, *could have*, *shouldn't have*.
- **Phrasal Verbs:** Learn and apply a block of 5 new phrasal verbs each week.

**Weekends (Production):** Pick a global topic (e.g. technology, environment). Read an article, write a 150-word opinion piece, then argue your position out loud recording yourself for 3 minutes.

### Mini Checkpoints

- [ ] **Challenge 1:** Listen to a 15-minute native podcast (e.g. BBC or TED) without pausing and summarize it orally at the end.
- [ ] **Challenge 2:** Hold a 15-minute monologue or conversation on an abstract topic without going blank for more than 5 seconds.
- [ ] **Challenge 3:** List 30 "basic" words (*good*, *bad*, *happy*) and successfully replace them with B2-level synonyms (*beneficial*, *detrimental*, *thrilled*).

---

## Stage 3: Months 5–6 — "The Strategy"

**Goal:** Exam technique, agility under time pressure, and natural fluency.

### Weekly Focus Routine

**Tue & Thu (45–60 min):** Polish expert-level details:
- **Inversion:** Advanced emphasis structures (e.g. *"Not only did I…"* / *"Hardly had I…"*).
- **Time management:** Short grammar exercises timed with a stopwatch.

**Weekends (Production):** Full mock exams — Reading or Listening sections from official exams (Cambridge, TOEFL, IELTS) under strict time limits. In Speaking, use professional transition connectors.

### Mini Checkpoints

- [ ] **Challenge 1:** Complete an official Reading and Use of English section in real time with more than 60% accuracy.
- [ ] **Challenge 2:** Write a 200-word argumentative essay in under 40 minutes with no dictionaries or translators.
- [ ] **Challenge 3:** Express an improvised opinion on a random topic for 2 continuous minutes with fluency and without mentally translating.
